Pop Art

Who: Andy Warhol, Roy Lichtenstein

What: Paintings and sculptures of culture objects and media stars, usually bright and colorful

When: Mid 1950's to Early 1970's

Where: Britain and the United States

Why: Pop art aimed to blur the lines between "high class" art and "low class" pop culture

Examples

Drowning Girl, Roy Lichenstein, 1963

Standard Station, Ed Ruscha, 1966

I Was a Rich Man's Plaything, Eduardo Paolozzi, 1947

Still Life with Mandolin and Guitar, Pablo Picasso, 1924

The Cafe Terrace, Diego Rivera, 1915

The Bargeman, Fernand Léger, 1918

Table by a Window, Jean Metzinger, 1917
